Coda in the Key of F2654hD4

In the story behind Diebold AccuVote voting machines scandal, what was ‘F2654hD4’?
  • a default DES key that was hard coded in the Diebold voting machines
When was the Digital Encryption Standard (DES) deprecated by the National Institute of Standards and Technology?
  • In the mid-1970s
List three vulnerabilities of the AccuVote source code that were identified by computer scientist Aviel Rubin
  1. AccuVote system anonymized the voting order w/ a linear congruential genreator (LCG) that didn’t work properly and was inappropriate for this purpose, thereby undercutting the principle of the secret ballot.
  2. In parts of the code that require cryptography, either the algorithms were incorrectly applied or not used at all.
  3. Diebold’s approach to key management was juvenile–the same encryption key was hardcoded into every voting machine.
List two deficiencies in the Diebold AccuVote system identified by Harri Hursti:
  1. Removable memory cards
  2. boot loader
How do Independent Testing Authorities (ITAs) validate voting machines?
  • Compare results by re-running election records with known outcomes or using canned datasets with well-defined data.
Why are the ITAs as they’re currently configured inadequate to validate election equipment?
  • It provides a functional analysis of the source code by impartial, skilled computer professionals.
Are computing professionals allowed to inspect the source code of voting equipment?
  • Chip design and circuit analysis aren’t part of the validation because both are proprietary. No objective source code review is undertaken by skilled computer scientists, unless there’s been an accidental leak.
What is the standard procedure for determining weaknesses/vulnerabilities in source code?
  • Running source code through a commerical-quality source code analyzer.
List three vulnerabilities of the Diebold AccuVote source code identified by the UC Berkeley computer scientists
  1. Array bounds violations
  2. Faulty input validation errors
  3. Buffer overruns and underruns
What models do computer scientists use to validate mission-critical systems like those used in aircraft, by NASA, etc?
  • High-confidence software development paradigms.
What do voter verifiable paper audit trails (VVPAT) verify?
  • voter verification of the most recent behavior of the particular voting machine
What is the weakness of the current implemntations of VVPAT?
  • added complexity and amount of work for election officials increases dramatically, allowing more room for machine malfunction due to human error from poll workers
What is the part of today’s digital voting systems that is most vulnerable?
  • there’s far too much slop in digital voting process at too many different levels. the way in which it is administered falls short in terms of ability to verify outcomes, to technically validate equipment’s source code.

Legislating Technology (Badly)

List two features accorded by mobile device ‘kill switches’
  1. Wiping
  2. Bricking
Name a category of criminal who would be opposed to kill switches that ‘brick’ the device
  • A petty thief
Name a category of a criminal who would be in favor of kill switches that ‘brick’ the device.
  • Major crime lords and terrorists
Name a cateogory of a criminal who would be opposed to kill switches that wipe data from the device.
  • Identity thief
Would telecommunication companies be likely to take a strong position on kill switch legislation?
  • No, since carriers have no incentive to pass the legislation in the first place.
What is the position of the tech sector on kill switch legislation?
  • Strongly oppose the kill switch legislation
What is the likely position of the mobile device manufacturers and vendors on kill switch legislation?
  • Are against it since they prefer owner-based initiatives and they would have to rework their mobile platforms; however, they have no problem with legislation that penalizes users who reprogram their phones whether they are stolen or not.
What group most strongly favors kill switch legislation?
  • (Mobile Device Owners) / Users
What is the source of the privacy implications of kill switch legislation?
  • The tech sector
Define 911 swatting
  • Reporting bogus life-threatening situations to a 911 dispatcher. The goal is to spur local SWAT or tactical law enforcement units into a response to the bogus threat.
What is the real cause of swatting and other 911 vulnerabilities?
