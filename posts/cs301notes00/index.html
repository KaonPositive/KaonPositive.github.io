James Surowiecki holds that there are three necessary condtions for wise crowds. List them

  2. independence

What does convergence theory have to say about the behavior of crowds over time?

  • Says that over time, groups will congerge toward ‘conditions of similarity’–differences will diminish.

List two twentieth-century scholars who held that crowds tend to be less rational and intellectual than their smartest members

Trolling Pathologies

What is the popular netizen refrain regarding trolling?

  • “don’t feed the trolls”

How does the article define ‘online trolling’?

  • It is the practice of anonymously interrupting normal and customary information exchange in order to lure the recipient into reacting to the message.

How is online trolling different from other interference technologies like signal jamming, network blocking, network filtering, etc.?

  • It is in trolling’s anonymity and pathological allure–trolling attempts to either engage or inflame the receiver, usually through misinformation.

What is one of the main goals of trolling?

  • engage or inflame the receiver, usually through misinformation.

Trolling is a part of the Internet’s ____ space.

  • negative

List two examples of pedestrian online trolling

  1. hit-and-run posting
  2. shitposting

Define kakistocracy

  • rule by the least competent

Define pathrocacy

  • rule by a maladjusted minority, primarily psychopaths and narcissists

List five different types of trolling defined in the article:

  1. Provocation Trolling - elict a particular response from one or more participants
  2. Social Engineering Trolling - incite participants to activities that they would normally not have undertaken
  3. Sport Trolling - gratify the troll (just for the fun of it)
  4. Nuisance Trolling - derail the thread of an online forum (blog, chatroom, etc.)
  5. Jam Trolling - disrupt a communication channel (trolling equivalent to a DDoS attack)

Why is online trolling so effective at manipulating public opinion?

  • because it easily escapes detection by the uninitiated

List and explain the 4 categories of trolling stimulus and response.

  1. ~TROLL/~TROLL: ordinary exchange
  2. ~TROLL/TROLL: indicating a troll/trollop that injects him/ herself into an otherwise ordinary exchange
  3. TROLL/~TROLL: sucker/victim— the troll “catches” the unwary victim off-guard and manipulates to effect
  4. TROLL/TROLL: troll warfare; provocative in its potential deviance, this has yet to mature
